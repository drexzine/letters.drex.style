If you read nothing else, read this.

## What Drex believes

**Style is a practice, not a purchase.** It is not a thing you buy and own. It is a
thing you *do* — repeatedly, imperfectly, in the company of other people doing it too.
You get better at it the way you get better at any craft: by showing up, by making
something, by being near people who have been at it longer.

**Bring your gifts into the world — together, not alone.** Drex exists to help people do
exactly that. We gather in small circles around a craft — mending, drawing, making zines,
reworking what we already own — and we make real things side by side. Nobody performs.
Nobody optimizes. The newcomer learns by being *near* the veteran, not by being lectured.

**Visible mend, made by humans together.** This is the whole posture in five words. Like
the sashiko menders who never hid their stitches, we leave the seam showing. The clumsy
first attempt, the unfinished piece, the work someone almost didn't bring — we don't
bury any of it. The visible mend is how a practice tells the truth about itself: that it
is made of real people, keeping real things, imperfectly, together.

## What these letters are

This is **the correspondence** — the public copy of the thinking behind Drex. Not a blog
in the content-marketing sense, and not a newsletter in the personal-brand sense. It's
closer to the letters that communities of practice have always written to keep going
across distance — the Republic of Letters, the Quaker epistles, the bottega's wall.

Three kinds of letter live here:

- **Canon** — how the thing is meant to work.
- **Vision** — what we're building and the order we build it in.
- **Lore** — where the practice came from and what it's becoming.

## Three places to begin

If you want **the belief** — start with the vision letter:
[The room is the product.](/2026/06/the-room-is-the-product/) We are not building an app
with community features. We are building a community of practice that happens to need
software. The distinction decides everything.

If you want **the lineage** — start with the lore letter:
[Where the stitches show.](/2026/06/where-the-stitches-show/) Why sashiko menders left
their stitches visible, and why we never hide the seam.

If you want **the whole system** — read the canon:
[How Drex holds the practice between rooms.](/2026/05/the-correspondence/) Two thousand
years of how communities of practice have actually been held across distance, and what
that means for how we write to you.

When you're ready, [pull up a chair](/join-online/) — or just start reading on
