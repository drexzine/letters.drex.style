Every few weeks someone asks me, kindly, what the app *does*. They want a <span class="m-strike m-ink">feature list</span>. I understand the instinct — it's how we've been taught to ask after software — but <span class="m-underline m-coral">the question has the order backwards, and the order is the whole thing</span>.
{% include m-note.html text="they ask 'what does it do?' — wrong first question" color="lazuli" side="right" %}

Drex is not an app that has a community attached. Drex is a <span class="m-circle m-grass">community of practice</span> that needs, among other things, some software. The room comes first. <span class="m-box m-coral">The room is the product.</span> Everything we build is in service of the room continuing — between sessions, across cities, across the months a person spends moving from the edge of a practice toward its center.
{% include m-mark.html type="star" color="bus" side="left" %}
{% include m-note.html text="→ this is the whole thesis. read it twice." color="coral" side="left" %}

{% include callout.html kind="for-drex" title="For Drex" body="If a feature does not help a specific person make something with other people, or help that making be seen, or help the next person find their way in — it is not a Drex feature. It is a distraction wearing a Drex sticker." %}

Here is what I mean by a <span class="m-circle m-lazuli">room</span>. A small number of people — <span class="m-underline m-bus">five to twelve</span> — in a space, making the thing they came to make, in the presence of others doing the same. A mending circle. A drawing table. A thrift haul laid out and reckoned with. <span class="m-scribble m-coral">Nobody is performing. Nobody is optimizing.</span> Someone who has done this for years sits next to someone doing it for the first time, and the newcomer learns not by being lectured but by being *near* — by watching how a veteran holds the cloth, by being handed the good scissors, by being asked, gently, to bring the thing they were afraid to bring.
{% include m-note.html text="learning happens by being NEAR, not by being taught" color="grass" side="right" %}
{% include m-mark.html type="arrow" color="lazuli" side="right" %}

That is not a metaphor for what software can simulate. That is <span class="m-box m-lazuli">the irreducible unit</span>. The technology's only honest job is to make more of those rooms possible and to carry what happens inside them to the people who weren't there yet.
{% include m-note.html text="the load-bearing phrase →" color="lazuli" side="left" %}
{% include m-mark.html type="exclaim" color="coral" side="left" %}

{% include pullquote.html text="A community of practice is not an audience you've gathered. It is a craft you are keeping alive together, and you happen to be doing it in the same place." %}

This is why our metric is <span class="m-strike m-coral">not subscribers</span> and <span class="m-strike m-coral">not downloads</span> and not the number every other company in our category reports proudly to its board. Our metric is <span class="m-hl m-happy">**bridge crossings**</span> — a stranger becoming a reader, a reader walking into their first room, a first-timer coming back, a returner bringing a friend. Each crossing is a person moving closer to the center of the practice. <span class="m-underline m-grass">A hundred thousand views that produce no crossing is a failure dressed as a success.</span> <span class="m-scribble m-lazuli">Twelve people who would come to every future session is the business.</span>
{% include m-note.html text="bridge crossings = the only number that matters" color="grass" side="right" %}
{% include m-mark.html type="check" color="grass" side="left" %}
{% include m-note.html text="say this out loud. 12 > 100k." color="coral" side="left" %}

The software will get good. We'll ship the things you'd expect us to ship. But I want to be precise about the order, because <span class="m-underline m-lazuli">the order is what keeps a company honest</span> when the <span class="m-circle m-coral">growth-chasing</span> instincts arrive — and they always arrive. We resist them by remembering that we did not set out to build software. We set out to <span class="m-scribble m-grass">keep a practice alive</span>, the way every craft worth practicing has been kept alive: in small rooms, among apprentices, by people who showed up and made something real.
{% include m-note.html text="they ALWAYS arrive. name them so we can resist them." color="bus" side="right" %}
{% include m-mark.html type="caret" color="lazuli" side="right" %}

<span class="m-box m-coral">The room is the product.</span> The app is how the room keeps going <span class="m-underline m-bus">when you've gone home</span>.
